PhoneGap US 2016 Summary

These is a summary of the PhoneGap Day US 2016 by SAP employee's Marc-Lester Tan, Xiaojun Feng and Marco Eidinger

The PhoneGap day consisted of two parts: Jan 28 (workshops) and Jan 29 (conference)

The PhoneGap day was held in Adobe Office, Lehi, Utah.

Full Agenda with workshop and conference schedule

What's PhoneGap

What is PhoneGap? PhoneGap is powered by Apache Cordova and provides commerical services (some are for free) on top of Cordova.

For example, the PhoneGap team released the The PhoneGap Developer. The idea is you can install Node.js and PhoneGap on your computer, download the PhoneGap Developer App on your phone, and start developing apps without installing native SDK's or worrying about signing code. You can't distribute these apps but it works well for development.

PhoneGap offers a cloud build service to produce distributable apps.

As Cordova is the foundation it definitely made sense to get in touch with the PhoneGap community to learn about common topics and latest trends.

PhoneGap Day Kickoff

Speaker: Michael Brooks

History of PhoneGap Day

  • 2010 as a small training in Portland Orgeon
  • 2011: 1st PhoneGap Day
  • 2012 & 2013: PhoneGap Day US (Portland) / EU (Amsterdam) => Apache Cordova takes off
  • 2014: PhoneGap Day US (San Francisco)
  • 2015: PhoneGap Day EU (San Amsterstam)

2015 and Apache Cordova: staying up-to-date with the 3 platforms (iOS, Android, Windows)

2015 and PhoneGap: Desktop App to reach more users than CLI and the mobile app for instant preview

2016 outlook for Apache Cordova: embracenpm more (get rid of config.xml and replace it with package.json)

Mind the Gap: Supercharge your PhoneGap workflow

Speaker: Eric Alli (@_ericalli)

Its a kind of motivational talk with the motivation for clean & sustainable code, how to organize your project as well as use automation whereever possible like

  • dependency management (e.g. Bower)
  • task runner for reccuring task (e.g. Grunt or Gulp)

It shows example how to use and benefit from Grunt by executing build tasks

It emphasized the use of everyday's native plugins to give your app a more native feeling: social shairing, maps, page transitions, progress indicators, action sheets, etc.

Automated App Testing: A pain or cure?

Speaker: Martin Poschenrieder (@mposchenrieder)

Encouragement to use automated testing and a little bit advertising for TestMunk (as speaker is CEO and founder of it).

Important remark that automated tests are most powerful when tey are used as part of your continous development. Development and test have to work together!

Example for good solid test suite framework: Page Object Framework from Selenium

Also have rather less, but stable tests!!!

Further resources: blog.testmunk.com

Finding the sweet spot - blending the best of native and web

Speaker: Shawn Jansepar (@shawnjan8)

Why to choose between web or native? Experience matters and not technology.

Experience from Mobify projects: building some components natively for smooth and natural look and feel (especially navigation items) or high-performance critical elements and the rest are web view. P.S.: Amazon is using this concept as well.

Specific handling for web parts by detecting in which context the web content is running. For example, Mobify app detects the "app" in 3 places: app class in CSS, isRunningInApp function in JS and appending info to user agent for server-side handling

Options:

  • Embedded PhoneGap (to build a native hbyrid instead of a web hybrid)
  • Astro from Mobify
